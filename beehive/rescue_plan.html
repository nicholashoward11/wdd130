Rescue Plan

So right about now you are probably wondering what the next step is. "What should I do to save Honeybees?" Luckily, there are some very simple things that can be done to make a huge difference.

The first and most obvious would be to stop using any form of pesticide or herbicide. These artificial chemicals are extremely poisonous to honeybees and are the main cause for their extreme decline. Your garden may not be as beautiful as it could be, but the entire world will thank you for your sacrifice.

The next thing actually is even more simple. Like all other creatures, they need to drink water. Getting a small bird bath from your local supply store and adding small rocks to serve as platforms for the bees to rest on is a wonderful way to help the bees do the work we need them to do. Creating a safe environment for bees is key in ensuring that they not only survive but thrive in the wild.

Some other things that anyone can do are donating to bee farms, planting flowers, or simply even avoiding swatting them! If everyone who visits this site and learns about the dire need for honeybees takes these small steps towards saving them, a real difference can be made. Together we can help bees make the recovery they need and preserve the delicious fruits and vegetables we all love so much. The underestimated most important step to all of this is… talk! Share this knowledge and these ideas with your family, friends, neighbors… anyone who will listen! This only works if we all work together, so speak up! Save the Bees!
